  4. Eugene

  • Population: According to estimates, Eugene will have a population of 175,685 by 2023, making it the third-biggest city in Oregon and the 151st largest city in the country.

  • The city of Eugene was founded in 1862 and was given that name in honor of its creator, Eugene Franklin Skinner.

  • State region: The Willamette Valley region of western Oregon is where Eugene is situated. Its location alongside the Willamette River and its proximity to Springfield to the east make it a desirable place to live.

  • Eugene is categorized as an urban city since it contains a mixture of residential, business, and industrial zones.

  • Average income level: Eugene's median household income is $55,418; this is marginally less than the $68,319 median for the entire state of Oregon. Even so, Eugene's cost of living is lower than the average for the country, which can make up for Eugene's lower income levels.
